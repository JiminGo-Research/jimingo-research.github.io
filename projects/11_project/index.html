Overview Electroplating facilities rely on cyclic hoist scheduling to transport parts between tanks within strict soaking time windows — a problem known as the Cyclic Hoist Designing and Scheduling Problem (CHDSP). In practice, most facilities still delegate scheduling to outsourcing companies, which is costly, opaque, and takes up to 24 hours per design. This project proposes ElectroTwin, a digital twin-driven decision-support framework that integrates an automated scheduling algorithm with visual analytics to enable non-expert facility operators to make informed, data-driven decisions.

This work is published as: ElectroTwin: A Digital Twin-Driven Framework for Designing and Scheduling of Electroplating Facilities, D. Lee, T. Noh, J. Go, I. Park, K. Kim International Journal of Precision Engineering and Manufacturing-Green Technology, 2026, DOI: 10.1007/s40684-026-00867-9

This project extends the algorithmic foundation built in our earlier work — the OOPP Engine and CTS algorithm — into a full digital twin system with GUI-based visual analytics.
